Introduction

Optical fibers work on the principle of Total Internal Reflection (TIR), where light is completely reflected inside a denser medium (core) when it strikes the boundary at an angle greater than the critical angle.

This property makes optical fibers ideal for transmitting light and images over long distances with minimal loss, even when bent.

Usage in Medical Applications

Usage in Medical Applications

Endoscopy

An endoscope is a medical instrument that uses optical fibers to view the internal parts of the body without surgery.

Usage in Medical Applications

Laser

  • Optical fibers deliver precise laser light to targeted tissues, enabling minimally invasive surgeries.
  • Used in dermatology (removing moles, tattoos), dentistry, and eye surgeries
