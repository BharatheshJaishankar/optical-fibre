Optical Fibre

An optical fibre is a cylinderical dielectric waveguide tha transmits light along its axis by the process of internal reflection. It is used as a medium for telecommunications and networking because it can guide light over long distances with minimal loss.

Physical Principle: TIR

Optical fibers function based on Total Internal Reflection (TIR).

Conditions for light propagation:

1. ncore>ncladding

2. Angle of incidence i>ic

Fiber Architecture

  • Core: High refractive index glass/plastic.
  • Cladding: Lower refractive index coating.
  • Buffer Jacket: Protective outer layer.

Multiple internal reflections allow light to travel through curved paths.

Technical Specification

The efficiency depends on the Critical Angle:

ic = sin-1 n2 n1

For medical-grade glass: n1 ≈ 1.55 and n2 ≈ 1.48

GI Endoscopy

Used to examine the digestive tract (esophagus, stomach, and duodenum).

Mechanism:

  • Fibers transmit high-intensity light into the body.
  • Return fibers capture the image and relay it to a CMOS sensor or eyepiece.
  • Enables detection of ulcers, tumors, and internal bleeding.

Arthroscopy

Minimally invasive joint surgery.

  • Extremely thin fiber bundles (1-2mm) are inserted into joints (knee, shoulder).
  • Allows orthopedic surgeons to view cartilage and ligaments directly.
  • Reduces "Open Surgery" trauma significantly.

Laser Lithotripsy

Treating Kidney Stones:

Optical fibers act as a conduit for high-energy Holmium lasers. The light energy is delivered directly to the stone, shattering it into fragments small enough to pass naturally.

Cancer Treatment

Photodynamic Therapy (PDT)

  • A photosensitizing agent is injected into the patient.
  • Optical fibers deliver a specific wavelength of light to the tumor.
  • The light activates the drug, which produces oxygen radicals that kill cancer cells.

Ophthalmology

Retinal Repair and Cataract Surgery

Fibers provide Cold Light (no heat transfer), which is essential when working inside the delicate structure of the human eye.

Future Perspectives

  • Smart Fibers: Real-time pH and Glucose sensing.
  • Remote Surgery: Fiber networks enabling robotic surgery.
